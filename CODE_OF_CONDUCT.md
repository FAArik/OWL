## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the OWL Academic Social Learning Platform pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on academic excellence and collaborative learning.

## Our Standards

### ‚úÖ Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

**Academic Excellence**
* Sharing knowledge freely and helping others learn
* Providing constructive feedback on academic work and ideas
* Respecting different learning styles and educational backgrounds
* Encouraging academic curiosity and intellectual growth
* Supporting students, educators, and researchers at all levels

**Community Collaboration**
* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other community members
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback

* Using welcoming and inclusive language
* Maintaining academic integrity in all discussions and contributions
* Providing proper attribution and citations when sharing academic content
* Being patient with newcomers and those learning new concepts
* Engaging in thoughtful, evidence-based discussions

* Deliberately spreading false information or conspiracy theories
* Engaging in flame wars or aggressive arguments

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and moderators are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction
**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public or private apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning
**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Academic Appeals Process

Given our academic focus, we provide an additional appeals process for educational disputes:

### Academic Misconduct Appeals
* Issues related to academic integrity may be reviewed by an academic panel
* Appeals must be submitted within 30 days of the initial decision
* The review will consider academic standards and educational best practices
* Decisions aim to be educational rather than purely punitive

### Procedure
1. Submit a formal appeal explaining your position
2. Provide relevant evidence or context
3. Attend a review meeting if requested
4. Receive a final decision with educational recommendations
